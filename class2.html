5:30 5 min
Warm-up Greet & review from last class
Greet each student by name. Ask: "How are you?" — encourage them to answer in English. Quickly review last week: "Do you remember numbers 1–10? Let's say them together!" This builds confidence and continuity.
Bienvenida · Repaso de la clase anterior
5:35 15 min
Reading A simple story — "Maria's Morning"
Read the story aloud first while students follow along. Then read sentence by sentence, asking students to repeat. Finally, ask volunteers to read one sentence each.
Lectura · historia corta en inglés simple
📄 Maria's Morning
My name is Maria. I wake up at 7 o'clock in the morning.
I go to the kitchen. I make coffee. The coffee is hot and good.
I eat breakfast. I eat bread and an egg.
I go to work at 8 o'clock. I take the bus.
The bus is full. I stand. I am tired, but I am happy.
At work, my boss says, "Good morning, Maria!" I say, "Good morning!"
I work hard. At 5 o'clock, I go home. I am hungry.
I cook dinner. I eat with my family. I love my family.
At night, I go to sleep. It is a good day.
Mi nombre es María. Me despierto a las 7 de la mañana. Voy a la cocina. Hago café... (historia completa en el papel para los estudiantes)
📋 Comprehension Questions — Ask Aloud
  1. What time does Maria wake up?
  2. What does Maria eat for breakfast?
  3. How does Maria go to work?
  4. What does Maria do at night?
  5. Is Maria happy? (Yes / No)
📋 Assess: Can students follow the story as you read? Can they answer simple questions? Note who tracked well vs. who got lost — that's your reading comprehension baseline.
5:50 15 min
Vocabulary 5 common daily words
Introduce each word with a picture or action. Say the word, have students repeat it. Show a simple sentence. Then go through all 5 and say: "Point to the word for ___."
Vocabulario · 5 palabras del día a día
Word / Palabra Meaning Example Sentence
eat
comer
 to put food in your mouth I eat breakfast every morning.
drink
beber / tomar
 to have water, juice, or coffee I drink water every day.
go
ir
 to move from one place to another I go to work at 8 o'clock.
need
necesitar
 something important, you must have it I need water. I need help.
want
querer
 something you would like to have I want coffee, please.
💡 Quick game: Call out the Spanish word and have students call back the English word. Fast and fun!
6:05 18 min
Speaking Real-life situations — "What do you say?"
Present each situation. Act it out yourself first. Then ask students to try — even just one or two words is great. Praise every attempt. There are no wrong answers.
Hablar · situaciones de la vida real
Situation 1
🛒 You are at the store. You want water.
The cashier asks: "Can I help you?" — What do you say?
Starter: "I want water, please." / "I need water."
Estás en la tienda y quieres agua. ¿Qué dices?
Situation 2
🏥 You are at the doctor. You don't feel good.
The doctor says: "How are you today?" — What do you say?
Starter: "I don't feel good." / "I am sick." / "My head hurts."
Estás en el médico. No te sientes bien. ¿Qué dices?
Situation 3
🚌 You are on the bus. You don't know where to get off.
You need to ask someone for help. — What do you say?
Starter: "Excuse me. I need help, please." / "Where is ___?"
Estás en el autobús y no sabes dónde bajarte. ¿Qué dices?
📋 Assess: Who tried to speak? Who needed the starter phrase? Who made up their own sentence? Note confidence levels — this is your speaking progress tracker.
6:23 7 min
Wrap-up Review + "See you Thursday!"
Point to each vocab word and ask the class together: "What is this word?" — Then say: "Today we read a story. We learned 5 words. We practiced speaking." Go around and say goodbye to each student by name. Remind them of the class website and your phone number.
Repaso · "¡Hasta el próximo jueves!"
💡 After class: Update your notes for each student on reading ability, speaking confidence, and vocabulary retention. You now have two data points — look for growth.
